## High-Surface-Area Catalyst Support for Advanced Reactions

Sepiolite's combination of high specific surface area (240 m²/g), thermal stability to 500°C, and tuneable surface chemistry makes it an outstanding catalyst support and carrier material. Researchers and industrial chemists use sepiolite to anchor metal nanoparticles, enzymes, and organocatalysts — achieving high dispersion, long catalyst life, and easy recovery in reactions ranging from petrochemical hydrogenation to pharmaceutical synthesis.

With 240 m²/g of BET surface area and a unique channel structure, sepiolite provides abundant anchoring sites for catalytic metals such as platinum, palladium, nickel, copper, and iron. Unlike organic supports that decompose above 200°C, sepiolite maintains its structure and catalytic metal dispersion at temperatures up to 500°C, enabling use in Fischer-Tropsch synthesis, methane reforming, and catalytic combustion.

Sepiolite's surface can be tailored through acid activation (increasing surface area to 400+ m²/g), organofunctionalisation, or metal impregnation. The mild, neutral surface is also ideal for immobilising lipases, laccases, and proteases — supporting biocatalysis with multi-cycle reuse. Environmental applications include automotive exhaust treatment (NOx reduction), industrial VOC destruction, and photocatalytic water purification.

The fibrous morphology creates a self-filtering catalyst bed: after reaction, catalyst-loaded sepiolite settles rapidly and can be recovered by simple filtration, reducing precious metal losses and simplifying process design. We supply purified, acid-activated, and surface-modified grades, with pelletised 3–5 mm extrudates available for fixed-bed reactor systems.
