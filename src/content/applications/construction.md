## Natural Insulation, Fireproofing & Workability Enhancement

Sepiolite brings unique advantages to the construction industry through its extremely low thermal conductivity (0.06 W/m·K), inherent fire resistance (A1 classification under EN 13501-1), and remarkable ability to improve the workability of cement, lime, and gypsum-based products. From insulating boards to fireproof coatings to heritage restoration mortars, sepiolite enhances building material performance while maintaining a fully natural, non-toxic composition.

Adding 3–8% sepiolite to cement or lime mortars dramatically improves workability, open time, and sag resistance. The fibrous particles create a thixotropic network that allows easy spreading while preventing slump on vertical surfaces. The mineral is particularly valued in the restoration of historic lime-based structures, where it improves the working properties of traditional hydraulic lime mortars without introducing modern chemicals.

Sepiolite is inherently non-combustible and retains structural integrity at temperatures exceeding 1000°C, making it ideal for passive fire protection systems, fireproof coatings, and fire-rated partition boards. Sepiolite-filled concrete and gypsum products are 20–30% lighter than conventional equivalents at comparable strength, reducing structural load, transportation costs, and installation labour.

Unlike many synthetic insulation materials such as XPS, EPS, and spray foam, sepiolite contains no volatile organic compounds, no CFCs, no HFCs, and produces zero toxic emissions during fire. It is a 100% natural mineral that can be safely disposed of at end-of-life, supporting sustainable construction targets across Europe and the Middle East.
