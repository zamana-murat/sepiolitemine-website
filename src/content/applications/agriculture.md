## Cultivating Better Soil with Sepiolite

Modern agriculture faces increasing pressure from climate variability, soil degradation, and the need to produce more food with fewer inputs. Sepiolite, a hydrated magnesium silicate mineral, offers a natural and proven solution to several of these challenges simultaneously.

When incorporated into agricultural soils, sepiolite's unique fibrous structure with channels measuring 3.7 × 10.6 angstroms acts like a microscopic sponge. It absorbs water and dissolved nutrients during irrigation or rainfall, then releases them gradually as plants demand. This single property addresses irrigation efficiency, nutrient loss, and drought resilience all at once.

For fertiliser manufacturers, sepiolite serves as an ideal carrier matrix. Its high surface area (200–300 m²/g) allows it to hold significant quantities of nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, and trace elements, while its mineral nature ensures complete plant safety with no residual contamination. Farmers benefit from reduced fertiliser application rates and improved nutrient use efficiency.

Beyond soil amendment and carrier applications, sepiolite finds use in seed coating, animal feed formulations, and agrochemical delivery systems. As a 100% natural mineral with EU and USDA organic compatibility, it fits seamlessly into both conventional and sustainable farming systems.
