## Engineered for the Toughest Downhole Conditions

Our drilling grade sepiolite is a specially processed rheology modifier designed for oil and gas drilling fluid applications. Unlike bentonite, sepiolite maintains its viscosity-building properties in saturated salt solutions and at temperatures exceeding 200°C — conditions that cause bentonite to flocculate and lose effectiveness.

The product meets API Specification 13A requirements for drilling-grade sepiolite and is routinely used in geothermal wells, deep offshore operations, and salt dome drilling. Its unique fibrous structure creates a three-dimensional network in drilling fluid that provides excellent hole-cleaning and cuttings suspension.

Drilling grade sepiolite also contributes to wellbore stability by forming a thin, low-permeability filter cake on the borehole wall. This reduces fluid loss to the formation and helps maintain wellbore integrity in problematic shale and unconsolidated sand intervals.

We supply drilling grade sepiolite to major oilfield service companies and national oil companies worldwide. Our product is backed by detailed technical data sheets and field performance reports for specific well conditions upon request.
